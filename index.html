ReaLiDAR: Material-Aware LiDAR Simulation for 3D Perception in Autonomous Driving

Abstract

LiDAR simulation is essential for developing and validating Autonomous Driving Systems (ADS), enabling controlled evaluation across diverse and safety-critical scenarios. Existing LiDAR simulation methods either rely on single-bounce ray casting or learn point cloud distributions in a data-driven manner, without explicit guarantees of physical plausibility. As a result, they fail to capture light scattering behaviors governed by material properties, such as specular reflection, transmission, and multi-echoes, leading to a persistent sim-to-real gap.

In this paper, we present ReaLiDAR, a material-aware LiDAR simulation framework for 3D perception in autonomous driving. Built on radiance-based light transport theory, ReaLiDAR simulates laser beamâ€“material interactions using physically-based material reflectance models, tracing multi-bounce paths and computing the time-of-flight and intensity of each echo. It further incorporates beam divergence, intensity calibration, and echo selection to faithfully reproduce real sensor behavior.

We evaluate ReaLiDAR in 3 representative scenarios covering key ADS functionalities: automated parking, autonomous emergency braking, and urban navigation, using two commercial LiDARs. Under challenging conditions involving glass and specular surfaces, ReaLiDAR improves range and intensity accuracy by 13.1% and 28.4%, respectively, compared to existing methods. Moreover, object detection model trained on ReaLiDAR data achieves an 18% gain in average precision on KITTI over CARLA-trained ones, showing its effectiveness in narrowing the sim-to-real gap.

ReaLiDAR consists of 4 modules: Laser Beam Modeling, Light Transport Modeling, Material Reflectance Modeling, and Post-Processing.


We propose ReaLiDAR, a material-aware LiDAR simulation framework for autonomous driving. The framework models the full LiDAR sensing pipeline, from the emission and divergence of laser beams, through their propagation in free space, to material-dependent scattering at surfaces that ultimately generates echoes. ReaLiDAR presents the following key contributions:

i) To the best of our knowledge, ReaLiDAR is the first material-aware LiDAR simulation framework for 3D perception in autonomous driving. It accurately models key light scattering behaviors across a wide variety of materials, including Lambertian, reflective, retro-reflective, and transmissive materials.

ii) We incorporate key factors governing LiDAR sensing, including beam divergence, material properties, intensity calibration, range falloff, and echo selection, in order to enhance simulation accuracy.

iii) We seamlessly integrate ReaLiDAR into CARLA simulator, facilitating large-scale testing of LiDAR-based autonomous driving models under diverse scenarios.

iiii) We evaluate ReaLiDAR using two LiDAR sensors across three representative scenarios. Results show that ReaLiDAR achieves closer alignment with real-world scans in both range and intensity compared to existing methods. Furthermore, synthetic data generated by ReaLiDAR improves performance on downstream vehicle detection task, narrowing the sim-to-real gap compared to models trained on CARLA-generated data.
